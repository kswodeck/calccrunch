## How to Read Your Bill and Find the Problem

### Step 1: Check Your kWh Usage

Average US household: **886 kWh/month** (~$140/month at national average rates)

| Monthly Usage | Category |
|---|---|
| Under 500 kWh | Low (apartment, efficient) |
| 500-900 kWh | Average |
| 900-1,500 kWh | High (large home, older systems) |
| 1,500+ kWh | Very high (investigate immediately) |

### Step 2: Compare Month-Over-Month

A sudden spike usually means:
- Seasonal change (AC or heating kicking in)
- New appliance or behavior change
- Malfunctioning appliance (old fridge running constantly)
- Rate increase from your utility

## The Biggest Savings (By Impact)

### 1. Thermostat Adjustment (Saves 20-30%)

Every degree you adjust saves roughly 3% on heating/cooling:

| Change | Annual Savings |
|---|---|
| AC from 72 to 76 | $200 - $400 |
| Heat from 72 to 68 | $150 - $300 |
| Programmable thermostat | $100 - $200 |
| Smart thermostat (Nest, Ecobee) | $150 - $250 |

A smart thermostat pays for itself in 6-12 months and continues saving for years.

### 2. Water Heater Optimization (Saves 10-15%)

Your water heater runs 24/7 maintaining temperature whether you're using water or not:

- **Lower temperature from 140 to 120**: Saves $50-$100/year, also prevents scalding
- **Insulate the tank**: $20 blanket saves $30-$60/year
- **Insulate hot water pipes**: $10 in supplies, saves $20-$40/year
- **Switch to heat pump water heater**: Uses 60% less energy (saves $300+/year)

### 3. Lighting Overhaul (Saves 5-10%)

| Bulb Type | Watts (60W equivalent) | Annual Cost (5 hrs/day) | Lifespan |
|---|---|---|---|
| Incandescent | 60W | $10.50 | 1,000 hours |
| CFL | 13W | $2.30 | 8,000 hours |
| LED | 8W | $1.40 | 25,000 hours |

Replacing 20 incandescent bulbs with LEDs: saves ~$180/year. LEDs also produce less heat, reducing cooling costs in summer.

### 4. Phantom Loads (Saves 5-8%)

Devices that draw power when "off":

| Device | Phantom Draw | Annual Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Cable box/DVR | 20-30W | $25-$35 |
| Game console (standby) | 10-15W | $15-$20 |
| Computer (sleep mode) | 3-10W | $5-$15 |
| Smart speaker | 3-5W | $4-$7 |
| Microwave (clock display) | 3W | $4 |

**Fix**: Smart power strips ($25-$40) cut power to devices automatically when you turn off the TV or computer. One strip per entertainment center can save $50-$100/year.

<div class="callout-warning">

**The old fridge trap**: A refrigerator from 2005 uses about 500-700 kWh/year. A modern Energy Star model uses 300-400 kWh/year. If you have a second "beer fridge" in the garage that's 15+ years old, it could be costing $100-$150/year to run — just to keep drinks cold.

</div>

## Seasonal Strategies

### Summer (AC is 40-60% of bill)
- Close blinds on south/west-facing windows during peak sun
- Use ceiling fans (cost $0.01/hour vs $0.40/hour for AC)
- Run AC at 76-78 when home, 82 when away
- Cook outdoors or use microwave to avoid heating the kitchen
- Seal air leaks around windows and doors

### Winter (Heating is 40-50% of bill)
- Set thermostat to 68 when home, 62 when sleeping
- Use space heater in occupied room vs heating whole house
- Open south-facing blinds during day for free solar heat
- Add weatherstripping to exterior doors ($10, saves $30-$50/year)
- Reverse ceiling fan direction (clockwise pushes warm air down)

## The Free Fixes (Do These Today)

1. **Adjust AC/heat by 2-4 degrees** — you'll adapt in 3 days
2. **Unplug chargers and devices not in use** — or use power strips
3. **Switch to cold water for laundry** — 90% of washing machine energy heats water
4. **Run full loads only** — dishwasher and laundry machine are most efficient full
5. **Turn off lights in empty rooms** — obvious but consistently ignored
6. **Clean your AC filter** — dirty filter makes the unit work 15% harder
7. **Close vents in unused rooms** — don't heat/cool rooms nobody uses

## The Bottom Line

- **50-60% of your bill** is heating/cooling and hot water — focus there first
- **A 2-4 degree thermostat change** saves 6-12% with minimal discomfort
