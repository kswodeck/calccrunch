A ratio compares two (or more) quantities to show how they relate in size. To calculate a ratio, divide the two quantities by their greatest common factor (GCF) to simplify it — for example, 8:12 simplifies to 2:3 because both numbers divide evenly by 4. This one skill unlocks scaling recipes, mixing paint, reading maps, splitting bills fairly, and solving the "if this, then how much of that" questions that show up constantly in cooking, DIY projects, and word problems.

## What Is a Ratio?

A ratio expresses the relationship between two numbers, written as **A:B** (read "A to B"). If a bag of trail mix has 3 cups of nuts to 2 cups of dried fruit, the ratio of nuts to fruit is 3:2 — for every 3 units of nuts, there are 2 units of fruit.

Ratios can compare:
- **Part to part** — 3 cups nuts : 2 cups fruit
- **Part to whole** — 3 cups nuts : 5 cups total mix

Both are valid, so always check which one a problem is asking for.

## How to Simplify a Ratio

**Step 1: Find the greatest common factor (GCF)** of both numbers — the largest number that divides evenly into each.

**Step 2: Divide both sides by the GCF.**

**Example:** Simplify 18:24.
- Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18
- Factors of 24: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24
- Greatest common factor: 6
- 18 ÷ 6 : 24 ÷ 6 = **3:4**

| Ratio | GCF | Simplified |
|---|---|---|
| 8:12 | 4 | 2:3 |
| 18:24 | 6 | 3:4 |
| 15:25 | 5 | 3:5 |
| 21:14 | 7 | 3:2 |
| 100:60 | 20 | 5:3 |

If the two numbers share no common factor greater than 1 (like 7:9), the ratio is already in its simplest form.

## How to Scale a Ratio

Scaling a ratio means multiplying (or dividing) both sides by the same number — the relationship between them never changes. This is exactly how recipes get scaled up or down.

**Example:** A pancake recipe calls for a flour-to-milk ratio of 2:3 (2 cups flour to 3 cups milk). You want to make a batch 2.5x larger.
- Flour: 2 × 2.5 = 5 cups
- Milk: 3 × 2.5 = 7.5 cups
- New ratio: **5:7.5**, which still simplifies back down to 2:3

## How to Solve a Proportion (Find the Missing Value)

A proportion sets two ratios equal to each other: **A:B = C:X**. To solve for the missing value, cross-multiply and divide.

**Formula:** X = (B × C) ÷ A

**Example:** A recipe uses a 4:8 ratio of sugar to flour (in tablespoons). If you're using 6 tablespoons of sugar, how much flour do you need?
- Set up the proportion: 4:8 = 6:X
- Cross-multiply: 4 × X = 8 × 6
- Solve: X = 48 ÷ 4 = **12 tablespoons of flour**

This same cross-multiplication method works for map scales, unit price comparisons, mixing cleaning solutions, and paint color ratios — anywhere you know three of the four values in a proportion.

## How to Compare Two Ratios

To check if two ratios are equivalent (or find which is larger), convert each to a decimal by dividing A by B, then compare — or cross-multiply and compare the products.

**Example:** Is 3:4 the same as 6:8?
- Cross-multiply: 3 × 8 = 24, and 4 × 6 = 24
- The products are equal, so **3:4 = 6:8** — they're equivalent ratios.

**Example:** Which is a stronger coffee-to-water ratio: 2:15 or 1:8?
- 2:15 = 2 ÷ 15 = 0.133
- 1:8 = 1 ÷ 8 = 0.125
- 0.133 > 0.125, so **2:15 is the slightly stronger ratio**.

| Ratio A | Ratio B | Equivalent? |
|---|---|---|
| 3:4 | 6:8 | Yes |
| 1:2 | 4:9 | No |
| 5:10 | 1:2 | Yes |
| 2:3 | 3:5 | No |

## Ratios vs. Fractions: What's the Difference?

A ratio (A:B) and a fraction (A/B) simplify using identical math — find the GCF and divide both sides. The difference is what they represent: a fraction usually describes a part of a single whole (3/4 of a pizza), while a ratio compares two separate quantities that may not add up to a "whole" at all (a 3:4 ratio of cats to dogs at a shelter). ## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you simplify a ratio?**
Find the greatest common factor (GCF) of both numbers, then divide each side by it. 8:12 has a GCF of 4, so it simplifies to 2:3.

**How do you solve A:B = C:X?**
Cross-multiply and divide: X = (B × C) ÷ A. For 4:8 = 6:X, X = (8 × 6) ÷ 4 = 12.

**How can you tell if two ratios are equivalent?**
Cross-multiply both ratios — if A × D equals B × C, then A:B and C:D are equivalent.

**How do you turn a ratio into a percentage?**
Divide the first number by the second and multiply by 100. A 3:4 ratio is 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75, or 75%.

**Can a ratio have more than two numbers?**
Yes — a three-part ratio like 2:3:4 works the same way. Divide all three numbers by their shared GCF to simplify, or multiply all three by the same factor to scale.
