\## Why June Is the Perfect Time



You're halfway through 2026. The goals you set in January — save more, pay off debt, invest consistently — either have momentum or they don't. A mid-year checkup takes 30 minutes and tells you exactly where you stand while there's still time to course-correct.



Think of it like a halftime adjustment. The game isn't over. But if you don't check the score, you can't change your strategy.



\## The 30-Minute Checklist



\### 1. Net Worth Snapshot (5 minutes)



Open your bank accounts, retirement accounts, and debt balances. Calculate:



\*\*Assets\*\* (what you own):

\- Checking + savings accounts

\- Retirement accounts (401k, IRA, Roth)

\- Investment/brokerage accounts

\- Home equity (if applicable)



\*\*Liabilities\*\* (what you owe):

\- Credit card balances

\- Student loans

\- Car loan

\- Mortgage balance



\*\*Net Worth = Assets - Liabilities\*\*



Compare to January 1. Did it go up or down? By how much? If it increased, you're building wealth. If it decreased, something needs attention.



\### 2. Budget Reality Check (10 minutes)



Pull your bank/credit card statements for the last 3 months. Answer:



\- \*\*What's your actual monthly spending?\*\* (Not what you budgeted — what you actually spent)

\- \*\*Where did the money go?\*\* Look at your top 5 spending categories

\- \*\*Any surprise categories?\*\* Subscriptions creep, dining out, Amazon purchases



The 50/30/20 framework: Are your needs under 50%, wants under 30%, and savings at 20%+?



If you haven't tracked this, now is the time. Use our budget calculator to set realistic targets for the second half of the year.



\### 3. Savings Rate Assessment (5 minutes)



Calculate your actual savings rate for 2026 so far:



\*\*Savings Rate = (Total Saved ÷ Total Income) × 100\*\*



Include: 401(k) contributions, IRA, emergency fund additions, and any other savings.



\| Savings Rate | Rating |

\|---|---|

\| 20%+ | Excellent — on track for financial independence |

\| 15-19% | Good — meeting standard retirement recommendations |

\| 10-14% | Okay — room to improve, but you're saving |

\| 5-9% | Below target — look for areas to cut or earn more |

\| Under 5% | Needs attention — even 1% more makes a difference |



If you're behind your January goal, increase by 2% for the rest of the year. Automate it so you don't have to think about it.



\### 4. Debt Payoff Progress (5 minutes)



For each debt, check:

\- \*\*Starting balance (Jan 1):\*\* ___

\- \*\*Current balance:\*\* ___

\- \*\*Amount paid down:\*\* ___

\- \*\*On track for annual goal?\*\* Yes/No



If you're paying minimums only on high-interest debt, summer is a great time to accelerate. Put your tax refund remnants, bonus, or one month of reduced spending toward the highest-rate balance.



\### 5. Investment Growth Check (5 minutes)



Log into your retirement and investment accounts:



\- \*\*Are you still contributing the planned amount?\*\* (Did you reduce after January motivation faded?)

\- \*\*Have you increased contributions with any raise?\*\*

\- \*\*Is your allocation still correct?\*\* (No rebalancing needed unless you're 10%+ off target)



Don't panic about market fluctuations. If you're investing for 10+ years, short-term dips are irrelevant. The question is: are you consistently adding money?



Use our compound interest calculator to see where you'll be by December if you stay on track.



\## Red Flags to Watch For



\- \*\*Savings rate dropped below 10%\*\* — Lifestyle creep is real. Audit subscriptions and discretionary spending.

\- \*\*Credit card balance increasing\*\* — You're spending more than you earn. Stop the bleeding before summer vacation spending makes it worse.

\- \*\*No emergency fund progress\*\* — If you still don't have 3-6 months of expenses saved, make this priority #1 for July-December.

\- \*\*Skipping retirement contributions\*\* — Every month you skip costs you thousands in future compound growth.



\## Quick Wins for the Second Half of 2026



1. \*\*Automate one new savings transfer\*\* — even $50/month adds $300 by December

2. \*\*Cancel 2-3 unused subscriptions\*\* — average person has $200/month in subscriptions

3. \*\*Increase 401(k) by 1%\*\* — you won't notice it in your paycheck

4. \*\*Set one debt payoff target\*\* — pick the smallest balance and eliminate it by December

5. \*\*Schedule this checkup again for September\*\* — quarterly reviews beat annual panic



\## The Bottom Line



You don't need a financial advisor for this. You need 30 minutes, honest numbers, and willingness to adjust. The people who build wealth aren't the ones who set perfect goals in January — they're the ones who check in, course-correct, and stay consistent.



Half the year is left. That's plenty of time to finish strong.
