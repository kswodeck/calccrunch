## The Quick Math

The average American spends **$13-$17 per meal eating out**. A meal-prepped lunch or dinner costs **$3-$6 per serving**. That's a 60-75% reduction per meal.

For someone eating out for lunch and dinner 5 days a week:
- Eating out: ($15 x 2 meals x 5 days) = **$150/week**
- Meal prepping: ($4.50 x 2 meals x 5 days) = **$45/week**
- **Weekly savings: $105**
- **Monthly savings: $420**
- **Annual savings: $5,040**

Even if you only meal prep lunches and still eat out for dinner, you save $2,500+ per year.

| Task | Time |
|---|---|
| Weekly meal planning | 15-20 minutes |
| Grocery shopping | 30-45 minutes |

### Compare to eating out:
- Driving/walking to restaurant: 10-15 min
- Waiting for food: 10-20 min
- Eating: 20-30 min
- Total per meal: **40-65 minutes**
- 10 meals out per week: **7-11 hours**

Meal prepping actually **saves time** compared to eating out daily. You spend 3 hours once instead of 1+ hour per meal, five times.

## The Hidden Savings People Miss

### 1. Reduced Food Waste
The average household throws away **30-40% of purchased food** ($1,500/year). Meal prepping with a plan reduces waste to under 10% because you buy exactly what you need.

### 2. Fewer Impulse Purchases
Without a meal plan, grocery trips average 20-40% impulse buys. A list-based trip cuts this to under 10%.

### 3. Less Delivery Fees and Tips
A $15 meal becomes $22-$28 after delivery fees, service charges, and tip. Eliminating 3 deliveries per week saves $40-$60/week in fees alone.

### 4. Health Cost Reduction
Home-cooked meals average 500-700 fewer calories than restaurant meals. Long-term, the health benefits translate to fewer medical bills and better energy.

## Start Simple: The Beginner Plan

Don't try to meal prep 21 meals in your first week. Start here:

**Week 1**: Prep lunches only (5 portions of one recipe)
**Week 2**: Add breakfast (overnight oats or egg muffins)
**Week 3**: Add 3 dinners
**Week 4**: Full week if you want, or stay at Week 3 level

<div class="callout">

**The 2-recipe rule**: Start with just 2 recipes per week. Cook each in bulk. Variety comes later — consistency comes first. Chicken + rice + veg in 5 containers is boring but it works, saves money, and takes 45 minutes.

</div>
