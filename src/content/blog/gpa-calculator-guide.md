## The 4.0 GPA Scale

Most US high schools and colleges use the 4.0 scale:

| Letter Grade | Percentage | GPA Points |
|---|---|---|
| A+ | 97-100% | 4.0 |
| A | 93-96% | 4.0 |
| A- | 90-92% | 3.7 |
| B+ | 87-89% | 3.3 |
| B | 83-86% | 3.0 |
| B- | 80-82% | 2.7 |
| C+ | 77-79% | 2.3 |
| C | 73-76% | 2.0 |
| C- | 70-72% | 1.7 |
| D+ | 67-69% | 1.3 |
| D | 63-66% | 1.0 |
| F | Below 60% | 0.0 |

## How to Calculate Unweighted GPA

Unweighted GPA treats all classes equally regardless of difficulty.

**Formula**: Sum of (grade points) / number of classes

### Example: Fall Semester

| Class | Grade | Points |
|---|---|---|
| English | A- | 3.7 |
| Math | B+ | 3.3 |
| Science | A | 4.0 |
| History | B | 3.0 |
| Spanish | A- | 3.7 |

**GPA** = (3.7 + 3.3 + 4.0 + 3.0 + 3.7) / 5 = 17.7 / 5 = **3.54**

## How to Calculate Weighted GPA

Weighted GPA gives extra points for harder classes:

| Course Level | A | B | C |
|---|---|---|---|
| Regular | 4.0 | 3.0 | 2.0 |
| Honors | 4.5 | 3.5 | 2.5 |
| AP/IB | 5.0 | 4.0 | 3.0 |

### Example: Same Student, Weighted

| Class | Level | Grade | Weighted Points |
|---|---|---|---|
| AP English | AP | A- | 4.7 |
| Honors Math | Honors | B+ | 3.8 |
| AP Science | AP | A | 5.0 |
| History | Regular | B | 3.0 |
| Honors Spanish | Honors | A- | 4.2 |

**Weighted GPA** = (4.7 + 3.8 + 5.0 + 3.0 + 4.2) / 5 = 20.7 / 5 = **4.14**

The same grades give a 3.54 unweighted vs 4.14 weighted — a significant difference that colleges notice.

<div class="callout">

**Which do colleges use?** Most colleges recalculate your GPA using their own system. However, they see your transcript and know which classes were AP/Honors. A 3.5 with 8 AP classes is more impressive than a 3.9 with zero. Take challenging courses — a slightly lower GPA in harder classes is better than a perfect GPA in easy ones.

</div>

## Cumulative GPA (Multiple Semesters)

If your classes have different credit hours, weight them:

**Formula**: Sum of (grade points x credit hours) / total credit hours

### Example: College Semester

| Course | Credits | Grade | Quality Points |
|---|---|---|---|
| Calculus II | 4 | B+ (3.3) | 13.2 |
| Chemistry | 4 | A- (3.7) | 14.8 |
| English 102 | 3 | A (4.0) | 12.0 |
| Psychology | 3 | B (3.0) | 9.0 |
| Lab | 1 | A (4.0) | 4.0 |
| **Total** | **15** | — | **53.0** |

**Semester GPA** = 53.0 / 15 = **3.53**

To combine multiple semesters, add all quality points and divide by all credit hours attempted.

## What GPA Do You Need?

### For College Admissions

| University Tier | Typical GPA (Unweighted) |
|---|---|
| Ivy League / Top 20 | 3.9 - 4.0 |
| Top 50 | 3.7 - 3.9 |
| Top 100 | 3.5 - 3.7 |
| State University | 3.0 - 3.5 |
| Community College | 2.0+ (open admission) |

### For Graduate School

| Program | Minimum GPA | Competitive GPA |
|---|---|---|
| Medical School | 3.0 | 3.7+ |
| Law School | 3.0 | 3.5+ |
| MBA (Top 20) | 3.0 | 3.5+ |
| STEM Master's | 3.0 | 3.3+ |

### For Scholarships

| GPA | Scholarship Likelihood |
|---|---|
| 3.8+ | Excellent (merit scholarships common) |
| 3.5 - 3.7 | Good (many opportunities) |
| 3.0 - 3.4 | Some options (need-based more common) |
| Below 3.0 | Limited merit options |

<div class="callout-warning">

**GPA isn't everything.** Colleges use "holistic admissions" — test scores, extracurriculars, essays, and recommendations all matter. A 3.6 student with leadership, strong essays, and community service can beat a 3.9 student with no activities. That said, GPA is still the single most important academic metric.

</div>

## How to Raise Your GPA

### The Math of GPA Recovery

Raising a GPA is harder the more semesters you have (each new semester has less weight):

| Current GPA | Semesters Completed | Semester GPA Needed for 3.5 |
|---|---|---|
| 3.0 | 2 | 4.0 for one semester |
| 3.0 | 4 | 4.0 for two semesters |
| 3.0 | 6 | Extremely difficult |
| 2.5 | 2 | 4.0+ (may need summer courses) |

### Practical Strategies

1. **Prioritize high-credit courses** — an A in a 4-credit class moves your GPA more than an A in a 1-credit class
2. **Retake failed courses** — many schools replace the F with the new grade
3. **Use grade replacement policies** — some schools allow D/F repeats
4. **Take summer courses** — extra credits with focused attention
5. **Don't overload** — 12 credits with all A's beats 18 credits with B's/C's

## Common GPA Mistakes

1. **Ignoring credit hours** — a C in a 4-credit class hurts more than a C in a 1-credit class
2. **Not calculating before dropping** — know if a W is better than a C for your GPA
3. **Forgetting +/- matters** — the difference between B+ (3.3) and B- (2.7) is 0.6 points
4. **Not checking your school's specific scale** — some use +/- differently
5. **Comparing weighted to unweighted** — a 4.2 weighted isn't "better" than a 3.8 unweighted; they're different scales

## The Bottom Line

- **Unweighted GPA**: All classes worth 4.0 max, measures raw grades
- **Weighted GPA**: AP/Honors get bonus points, can exceed 4.0
- **Credit hours matter**: Weight your calculation by credits for accuracy
- **Colleges recalculate**: They care about your transcript more than a single number
- **Trajectory matters**: An upward trend (3.2 → 3.5 → 3.8) looks better than a flat or declining GPA
