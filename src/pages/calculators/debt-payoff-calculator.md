## How to Use This Calculator

1. Enter each of your **debts** with the name, current balance, interest rate (APR), and minimum monthly payment
2. Add or remove debt rows as needed (up to 15 debts)
3. Set the **extra monthly payment** you can put toward debt above your minimums
4. Click **Compare Strategies** to see the Snowball vs. Avalanche payoff comparison
5. Review the timeline, milestones, and detailed schedule to pick the best strategy for you

<div class="info-box" style="background: var(--color-highlight-blue); border-left-color: var(--color-primary-blue);">
  <h4>Snowball vs. Avalanche: Which Is Better?</h4>
  <p>
    <strong>Debt Snowball</strong> targets the smallest balance first. You get quick wins that build motivation 
    and momentum. Psychologically powerful, but you may pay slightly more in total interest.
  </p>
  <p>
    <strong>Debt Avalanche</strong> targets the highest interest rate first. This is mathematically optimal 
    and saves the most money overall, but it can take longer to see your first debt disappear.
  </p>
  <p>
    <strong>The best method is the one you stick with.</strong> If you need early wins to stay motivated, 
    Snowball might be better for you even if it costs slightly more. If you are disciplined and want to 
    minimize total cost, Avalanche is the way to go.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="info-box" style="background: var(--color-highlight-green); border-left-color: var(--color-green);">
  <h4>Finding Extra Money for Debt Payoff</h4>
  <ul style="margin: 10px 0; padding-left: 20px;">
    <li><strong>Cancel unused subscriptions</strong> - streaming services, gym memberships, apps</li>
    <li><strong>Sell items you no longer need</strong> - electronics, furniture, clothing</li>
    <li><strong>Reduce dining out</strong> - cooking at home can save $200-400/month</li>
    <li><strong>Pick up a side gig</strong> - freelancing, rideshare, tutoring</li>
    <li><strong>Negotiate bills</strong> - call providers for lower rates on internet, insurance, phone</li>
    <li><strong>Use windfalls wisely</strong> - tax refunds, bonuses, gifts toward debt</li>
  </ul>
  <p>
    Even an extra $50-100/month can shave months or years off your debt payoff timeline.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="info-box" style="background: var(--color-highlight-yellow); border-left-color: var(--color-warning);">
  <h4>Important Caveats</h4>
  <ul style="margin: 10px 0; padding-left: 20px;">
    <li>This calculator assumes <strong>fixed interest rates</strong>. Variable-rate debts (like credit cards) may change over time.</li>
    <li>It does not account for <strong>late fees, annual fees, or penalties</strong> for early payoff.</li>
    <li>Minimum payments on credit cards typically decrease as balance drops. This calculator uses a fixed minimum.</li>
    <li>Results assume you <strong>do not add new charges</strong> to any debt during the payoff period.</li>
    <li>Some loans have <strong>prepayment penalties</strong> - check your loan terms before making extra payments.</li>
  </ul>
</div>
